TYPE A JUDGING

The participant explicitly answered this question. The gold is what they actually said.

Use this rubric:
- correct: proxy's Response covers the gold's factual content (paraphrase is fine; exact wording not required) AND does not contradict the gold evidence.
- partial: proxy's Response covers SOME of the gold but misses a key fact, detail, or specific item the gold contains.
- wrong: proxy's Response contradicts the gold OR introduces specific content (names, tools, numbers) not in the gold evidence.
- abstained: proxy declined to answer ("I don't have enough information" or equivalent). For Type A this is the wrong behavior — the information was actually in the transcript.

Be liberal on paraphrase. "Used AI for cleaning data and building model skeletons" matches gold "We used AI to help clean data and also to build a basic skeleton".

Be strict on hallucinated specifics. If gold only says "AI" generically and the proxy says "ChatGPT", that is wrong, not paraphrase.
